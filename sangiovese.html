Sangiovese Wine Profile

  • Fruit: Fig, Tart Cherry, Strawberry, Red plum
  • Other Notes: Potpourri, Gravel, Roasted Pepper, Tomato, Clay, Brick, Thyme, Oregano
  • Oak: Yes, Usually light oak aging in oak barrels.
  • Tannins: High
  • Acidity: High

Tasting Sangiovese Wine

Sangiovese is typically quite savory. However, Sangiovese offers a truly wide range of tastes that can range from rounded and fruit-forward to very rustic and earthy. When trying a new Sangiovese, dedicate a moment to sit and smell it for a while. As you sit and sniff, you may begin to notice that the aromas move towards dried roses, cherries, and figs. This is especially true with older Sangiovese wines.
