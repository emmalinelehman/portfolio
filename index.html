Italian Red Wine is for everyone

Selecting an Italian red wine can be daunting.

Some sources suggest that there are roughly 500 or more different Italian wines! With 377 indigenous wine grapes in Italy and the existence of biotypes or sub-varieties, the number of Italian wines is suspected to be even larger. Fortunately, each of the 20 regions of Italy specialize in a couple primary wines and varietals. This offers a great starting place for those looking to explore.

The best Italian reds for beginners have a tendency to share these common characteristics:

  • They are packed full of bold and intense flavors
  • They are found at approachable price points (typically under $25 a bottle)
  • They put the focus on familiar fruity flavors and aromas

Eight Italian Red Wines Perfect for Beginners

  1. Sangiovese
  2. Montepulciano (the grape)
  3. Barbera
  4. Nero d’Avola
  5. Primitivo
  6. Valpolicella Blends
  7. Dolcetto
  8. My personal favorite, Nebbiolo
